"Olive Garden Breadsticks... But Better"

  • 2 teaspoons (6g) instant yeast
  • 1 cup (240g) water
  • 3 cups (450g) bread flour
  • 2 tablespoons (30g) granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons (8g) fine sea salt
  • 3 tablespoons(30g) good extra virgin olive oil

    • Toppings:

  • 1/4 cup (56g) melted butter
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, grated
  • flakey salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Mix in the yeast and lukewarm water and let sit for about 8 minutes
  2. In a seperate bowl add bread flour,granulated sugar, and fine sea salt and mix.
  3. Using a stand mixer with the paddle attachment on medium-low speed, add in half of the mixture of bread flour, granulated sugar, and fine sea salt with the yeast and lukewarm water mixture, and then add 3 tablespoons good extra virgin olive oil. Mix until thourgly combined
  4. Switch to a dough hook attachment and add the other half of the flour, sugar, and salt mixture. Mix together until smooth and elastic (about 2 to 3 minutes)
  5. Shape your dough into a ball and lightly grease a medium sized bowl with olive oil, stick the ball of dough in there and wrap it up with lightly greased plastic wrap or a damp towel.
  6. Let it rise for about an hour or until its doubled in size
  7. Lightly flour a work surface, dump your dough on it and cut the dough into 12 even pieces (55-60g). Let the pieces sit for 12 minutes under a damp tower or plastic wrap
  8. Shape your dough and let sit under a damp towel or plastic wrap for 45 more minutes
  9. Once they are done proofing place them in an oven set to 425'F for 13 minutes or until golden brown
  10. While the breadsticks are baking in a sauce pan heat 1/4 (56g) of butter, take off the heat and grate 2-3 cloves of garlic(you dont want to cook the garlic your just giving the butter a garlicy taste). Let that sit for about 30 seconds then strain the garlic out of the butter(obviously by putting it in another container)
  11. Remove the breadsticks after the timer is done and then immediately soak the breadsticks with the butter "sauce" and then finally add salt ontop.
